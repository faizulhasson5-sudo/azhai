How to Check Keyword Density: Step-by-Step Guide with Free Tools

2026-07-06 SEO Tutorial

Why Check Keyword Density?

Checking keyword density ensures your content is optimized without being over-optimized. Too many keywords triggers Google penalties. Too few means you are not targeting your topic effectively. The sweet spot is 1-2% for your primary keyword.

Step 1: Write Your Content First

Do not think about keyword density while writing. Focus on creating valuable, comprehensive content. Write naturally, using your keyword where it fits. Once your draft is complete, then check density.

Step 2: Use Our Free Keyword Density Checker

Go to our Keyword Density Checker. Paste your content into the input area. Click Analyze. The tool shows: total word count, keyword frequency for each term, density percentage for each keyword, and a visual chart of keyword distribution.

Step 3: Analyze the Results

Look at your primary keyword first. Is it between 1-2%? If higher, you are over-optimized. Remove some instances. If lower, add the keyword where it fits naturally. Check secondary keywords — they should be around 0.5-1%.

Step 4: Adjust and Recheck

Make adjustments, then recheck. Common fixes: remove keyword repetitions in consecutive sentences, replace some instances with synonyms, move keywords to more prominent positions (title, H1, first paragraph) instead of repeating in body text.

Step 5: Check Competitor Density

For comparison, paste competitor content into the same tool. See what density they target. This gives you a benchmark. If competitors average 1.5% and you are at 0.5%, you may need more keyword usage. If they are at 1% and you are at 3%, pull back.

Pro Tips

Check density for all variations of your keyword, not just the exact match. For "keyword density," also check "keyword frequency," "keyword usage," and "keyword percentage."
